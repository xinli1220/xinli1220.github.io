The Rower

This soundslide is about a rower of Kansas Crew at the University of Kansas. Kate Marples, a law school student now, has been on the crew for six years. She has won NCRA champions and a lot of other regional regattas with her partner. She is still training for regattas. Marples is also an assistant coach for the crew. She says she enjoys coaching new members and helping them making differences through their college lives. Rowing is a demanding sport, which requires tremendous working-out and practicing. Kate gets up at five o’clock every day from Monday to Friday to train others and herself. The desire to win is what drives her everyday, said Marples. The pictures are of her as a trainee, a coach, and a competent athlete. Pictures are taken in the boat house, on the lake and in Marples’ home last fall. Can We Talk

Xin directed and produced this seven-minute documentary when she was a senior. "Can We Talk" is a high school program in Lawrence, Kan. inspired by the book "Courageous Conversations about Race". The program promotes understandings of race and aims at closing the academic achievement gaps. This short documentart is produced by students of the University of Kansas about the program activities and it has helped high school students under race.

24 Hours Rock Climbing

Xin spent 30 hours on a ranch in Arkansas to film this anuual 24-hour rock climbing competition with her friends. Hundreds of dressed-up climbers from states all over the country join this fun and demanding competition. It was a tiring but exciting filming experience. How far would you go to enjoy and the things your passion resides? That is the question such activity raises.
